- AI-driven code optimizations create "intent erosion" - thousands of small, functionally correct improvements that cumulatively strip away emotional and purposeful design decisions

- Ship of Theseus problem: continuous micro-changes make products functionally better but emotionally flatter over time
- Example: customer service agent's intentional southern accent removed by optimization for "professionalism" destroys brand voice

## Useful Information

**Protecting Intent in Codebases**
- Encode intent directly in codebase alongside code (not external wikis or chat threads)
- Document why behind architectural choices, desired emotions, brand voice, user experience goals immediately after making decisions
- Place intent documents "in the path of builders" so anyone (human or AI) encounters them before making changes
- Current development processes have no mechanism to protect intent - not tested, not stored with code, not audited
