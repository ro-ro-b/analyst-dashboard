### 4. Options Flow as Conviction Arbiter — Signal vs Noise Framework — REINFORCED

**Status: REINFORCED** — Feb 24 provides clearest methodological case study

This pillar, implicit in earlier analysis, was made fully explicit in the Feb 24 session. Kochuba demonstrated a systematic methodology for determining whether market moves reflect institutional conviction or momentum day-trading noise.

**Three-layer filter methodology:**

**Layer 1 — Volume ratios:** Put/call volume ratios in affected sectors (software, digital payments) were below 1:1. For an actual "apocalypse," you would expect put volumes dramatically exceeding call volumes. Digital payments call volume was actually slightly elevated relative to 30 days.

**Layer 2 — Open interest change:** The key metric. Kochuba's "flow ratio" measures actual open interest change vs total volume traded. Johnson & Johnson example: 462K options traded, only 11K contracts changed open interest (2.4%). Open interest actually dropped by 800K contracts. When flow ratio is below ~10%, the volume is overwhelmingly day-trading churn, not conviction positioning. This applied across all software/payment names analyzed.

**Layer 3 — Flow Patrol proprietary data:** Breaks down trading by actor (hedge fund, market maker, bank, retail). Flow Patrol showed minimal hedge fund activity in software/payment names. The only material named trade was PayPal — 7,000 calls bought to open (bullish, not bearish) for Friday expiration. No apocalyptic put-buying from institutional actors.

**Conclusion framework:** "Everything you look at through all this options data is telling us that this software thing didn't move." The Satrini article generated viral sentiment and momentum day-trading, but zero institutional options positioning. "If I was to show you this chart and said what do you think happened in these sectors yesterday, you wouldn't have seen anything in the digital payment space."

**Methodological implication:** This framework provides a repeatable test for any viral narrative: does sentiment translate to positioning? When it doesn't (as with Satrini), the move is momentum/noise and mean-reversion is favored. When it does (as with Iran — where oil, airlines, and sector-specific puts all confirm), the move has institutional conviction and should be respected.

### 5. Zero DTE Is Reshaping Market Microstructure — EVOLVING

The launch of Monday/Wednesday single-stock 0DTE options in Mag7+iBit has been a major structural development. Tesla immediately captured 1.3M contracts/day (50% of total volume), Nvidia 985K (37.5%), Apple ~500K. However, 90%+ of this volume doesn't convert to open interest — high-frequency churn, not directional conviction. The "seek and destroy" pattern (market tags largest 0DTE position then mean-reverts) is a consistent intraday phenomenon.

**March 5-6 refinement:** Zero DTE serves as a stability mechanism — but one that recedes under stress. When VIX spikes and geopolitical tensions rise, zero DTE sellers back off because they won't risk selling puts if bombs could fly. This removes the mean-reversion buffer and allows convexity to kick in. Kochuba: "The data shows that when V spikes, zero DT goes away and zero DT is the stability mechanism." This dynamic explains why intraday 1% swings occurred but daily mean-reversion held — until it stops holding, which is the transition Kochuba was flagging.

**Zero DTE risk asymmetry:** If short a 30-day put, risk exposure spans 30 nights of potential escalation. Zero DTE has no overnight exposure — "you can play in that sandbox with relatively lower risk." This structural advantage explains persistent 0DTE put-selling in geopolitical crises and the mean-reversion floor it creates. But when even intraday risk becomes too high (direct conflict escalation), the floor disappears.

### Signal vs Noise Filter (Crystallized Feb 24)

```
Event: Viral narrative / sharp price move

Layer 1 — Volume ratios:
  → Put/call volume ratio > 1.5? → Possible conviction
  → Put/call volume ratio < 1.0? → Noise / day-trading

Layer 2 — Open interest change:
  → Flow ratio (OI change / total volume) > 20%? → Positioning
  → Flow ratio < 10%? → Churn, no conviction
  → OI declining despite heavy volume? → Closing, not opening

Layer 3 — Actor identification (Flow Patrol):
  → Hedge fund activity in affected names? → Conviction
  → Only market maker / bank / retail? → Structural, not directional
  → Long-dated (5-6mo+) positions opened? → High conviction signal
  → Friday/weekly expirations only? → "Big swinger" day trade, ignore

Verdict: All three layers must confirm for institutional conviction.
Satrini selloff (Feb 24): Failed all three → noise → mean-reversion favored
Iran conflict (Mar 2-6): Passed all three → signal → respect positioning
```
