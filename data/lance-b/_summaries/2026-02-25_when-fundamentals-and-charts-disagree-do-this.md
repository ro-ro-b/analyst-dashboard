## Key Points
- Fundamentals assess what something should be worth; technicals assess what participants are actually doing right now — the gap between them is where traders get hurt
- Three ways fundamentals damage traders: (1) most lack the skill to have a true fundamental edge vs. sector specialists with decade-long networks and models, (2) time-frame mismatch — fundamentals resolve over quarters/years while traders operate intraday/weekly, (3) fundamental bias causes traders to ignore stops, fight trends, and miss setups
- MSTR case study: traded at 1.9x net asset value of BTC holdings; fundamental shorts expecting mean-reversion got squeezed as premium widened further before eventually compressing to 1.4x — "price can stay disconnected from fundamentals far longer than a trader can stay solvent"
- BMR, Beyond, and Circle (2025) examples of where fundamental context (share offerings, bankruptcy dilution, lockup expiry) was useful for understanding *why* a spike would reverse — but only tradeable when technicals confirmed

## Notable Claims or Positions
- "The chart is always right, not your opinion, not your valuation, not what you think should happen" — Brightstein wrote this daily on his morning report card for years
- Most traders' fundamental analysis is "fundamental vibes" — a metric, narrative, or Twitter thread that creates false confidence
- Core rule: fundamentals may inform context but are never allowed to override price action
- Best trades occur when fundamental view + technical confirmation align — "swing the bat hard" when both agree

## Actionable Takeaways
- Use fundamentals narrowly: catalyst research, understanding positioning/participants, and identifying who's trapped — not for setting price targets or overriding technical signals
- If fundamentals and technicals conflict, follow the chart and manage risk — your P&L grades timing and risk management, not long-term correctness
- Size up only when fundamental thesis and price action align; otherwise, respect the trend regardless of valuation opinion
