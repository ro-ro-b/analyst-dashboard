## Key Points
- 3-step loss recovery plan: (1) objectively review/write up the trade while fresh, (2) propose specific solutions, (3) build and implement safeguards — plus bonus step 4: "be a goldfish" and move on once the work is done
- Step 1 review framework: screenshot intraday + daily chart, describe what happened, capture emotions before/during/after, assess market context, classify whether it was in your playbook, check if risk rules were followed, identify contributing factors (FOMO, poor sleep, hot streak)
- Avis Group example: Brightstein lost $2M+ in ~1 hour on a reversal trade that was in his playbook; root cause traced through review was not execution but sizing, which was driven by anchoring to an outcome goal
- Step 2 — solutions must be specific and actionable: e.g., "write down intraday + daily trend before any trade," "enforce a name-that-play rule," "implement firm session loss limit with mandatory cooldown"
- Step 3 — implementation safeguards: use broker-level daily risk limits, AI tools to auto-lock-out after max loss, habit changes for sleep/health; solutions without implementation are useless
- Best traders impress with both urgency and thoroughness of their post-loss solutions — this separates them from average traders who identify problems but skip the fix

## Notable Claims or Positions
- "Trading is you versus you" — recovery quality from losses determines future wins; losses are tuition, so extract maximum learning
- It almost never happens that there's truly nothing to learn from a losing trade — even "correct" losses usually reveal improvement areas
- Rumination after completing the review process is counterproductive — once the lesson is learned and safeguards are set, give yourself permission to move on

## Actionable Takeaways
- After any significant loss: wait until productive headspace, then write the full trade review covering chart, emotions, context, playbook classification, and contributing factors
- For each identified cause, write a concrete "if X, then Y" solution rule — then implement it at the system level (broker limits, AI lockout tools, routine changes) rather than relying on willpower
- Treat every loss as tuition with a mandatory learning extraction — but once the work is done, stop dwelling and focus forward on process
