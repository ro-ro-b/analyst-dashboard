## Key Points
- "No man's land" = price action stuck mid-range with contracting volatility — looks tradable but has negative expected value; most paper-cut losses accumulate here
- Common traps: (1) after a stock makes a leg + counter-leg then chops in contraction, (2) consolidation after a breakout leg, where traders prematurely position for the next move
- Paper-cut losses compound psychologically — they shrink risk tolerance and cause traders to skip the actual high-EV setup when it arrives
- Exceptions: fresh breaking news invalidates prior range; clear S/R level within the range can be traded but at reduced size
- Real example from S&B Capital mentoring: trader shorted Nicola twice in no man's land (lost $2,500), then skipped the actual breakdown — net impact ~$5K (realized loss + missed profit)
