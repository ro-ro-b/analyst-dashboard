- Most traders are not limited by intelligence — they're limited by reps; they try to think their way into skill instead of drilling through practice
- Trading is a performance profession comparable to elite athletics — hobby-level intensity cannot produce pro-level outcomes
- The comfortable work traders gravitate toward (catalyst research, indicator tweaking) is usually not the real constraint; the uncomfortable constraint (sizing, psychology, risk management) is where leverage lives

## Actionable Takeaways
- Run a constraint sprint: 30 days, one goal, one metric, one behavior change at superhuman volume (e.g., 50 replay reviews/day focused on entries, or 100 sizing practice reps/day)
- Prioritize via data: list all improvement areas, estimate time-to-solve, probability of solving, and expected P&L impact — the winner becomes the singular focus until solved
- Protect focus hours ruthlessly: block time for deep work (chart write-ups, backtesting, review), eliminate notifications/scrolling, and be honest about whether your commitment matches your ambition
