## Key Points
- Options selling = insurance analogy: you pocket premium most of the time, but catastrophic payouts (hurricanes) wipe out years of collected premium — fat-tail events occur far more often than sellers assume
- High win rate ≠ edge: a strategy can win 90% of the time and still have $0 expected value (win $1 90% of the time, lose $9 10% of the time); further OTM options amplify this to 99%/$1 wins vs. 1%/$99 losses
- Calls out "Invest with Henry" by name: counts premium as income but excludes assignment losses when stocks decline — calling put premium "income" is misleading since it's compensation for risk, not ownership yield
- Covered calls cap upside in exchange for small premium — you systematically sell your best outcomes; SanDisk example of massive asymmetric winner that would have been called away
- Zero DTE options can move 100x against sellers on sharp intraday moves; United Healthcare lost 50%+ in months in 2025 — "boring blue chip" puts are not safe
- Psychological trap: consistent small wins condition complacency → traders increase size, sell closer strikes, lower standards → steamroller eventually hits

## Notable Claims or Positions
- Markets are efficient enough that blindly selling options has negative expected value after fees — you need a specific edge to profit, not just a strategy
- Best time to sell options is after the catastrophic event (analogous to insurers raising premiums post-hurricane), when implied volatility is blown out and risk is overpriced
- "There is no such thing as easy money in the market. No such thing ever."

## Actionable Takeaways
- Never conflate high win rate with positive expected value — always model the full distribution including tail losses before sizing an options selling strategy
- If selling options, do so selectively after volatility spikes when premium overcompensates for risk — not as a steady income strategy in low-vol environments
- Ask the stress test question before any premium-selling strategy: "What happens in a real crash — 30% market drop in a week, IV doubles, safe stock gaps 40% on earnings?"
