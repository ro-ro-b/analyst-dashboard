## Key Points
- MACD uses 12-period and 26-period exponential moving averages; MACD line = 12 EMA minus 26 EMA
- Signal line is 9-period EMA of the MACD line; histogram shows distance between MACD and signal lines
- MACD is lagging indicator that reacts to price changes, not predictive; crossovers often occur after significant moves already completed
- Divergence signals potential trend exhaustion: price makes new high while MACD makes lower high (bearish) or price makes new low while MACD makes higher low (bullish)
- SanDisk chart example shows MACD histogram approaching zero after months of uptrend, suggesting momentum stalling
- Bitcoin example (October 10th breakdown) shows MACD histogram remained negative throughout downtrend despite price bounces
