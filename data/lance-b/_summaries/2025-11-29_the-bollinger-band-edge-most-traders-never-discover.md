## Key Points
- Bollinger Bands consist of 20-period simple moving average with two standard deviation lines; bands not used as exact entry/exit levels
- Three primary applications: pendulum/mean reversion concept, overextension measurement, and volatility contraction detection
- Palantir trade example (August 20, 2025): stock went from steady strong +6 sentiment above upper band to -8 panic at lower band, dropping 20% in final two days; entry timed at 20-period MA break
- UA example (October 14, 2025): peaked after extended run above upper band with capitulatory volume signaling top; short trade structure using right side of V pattern yielded $3+ profit per share
- Contracting bands signal "no man's land" low-volatility periods requiring trade avoidance or significantly reduced position sizing (MSTR example November 21, 2024)

## Notable Claims or Positions
- Best capitulation plays occur when price meaningfully extends beyond Bollinger Bands after already trending in that direction
- Contracting bands during consolidation do not indicate overextension; only use bands as overextension gauge in trending markets with expanding bands
- Confluence of multiple systems required for edge: capitulatory volume, intraday patterns, moving average breaks, and Bollinger Band positioning must align

## Actionable Takeaways
- Avoid trades when Bollinger Bands contract during mid-range consolidation; wait for expansion and range break
